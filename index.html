Understanding the Greeks

Δ
Delta
Measures how much the option price changes for every ₹1 move in the underlying. Think of it as the "speed" of the option price.
Call: 0 to +1 · Put: −1 to 0
Γ
Gamma
Rate of change of Delta. Highest for ATM options near expiry. High gamma means your delta exposure can shift rapidly — the "acceleration" of the option.
Always positive for option buyers
Θ
Theta
Time decay per calendar day. Every day that passes, the option loses this much value (all else equal). Option sellers collect theta; buyers pay it.
Almost always negative for buyers
ν
Vega
Change in option price per 1% increase in implied volatility. Identical for calls and puts. Both benefit when India VIX rises (long vega position).
Always positive for option buyers
ρ
Rho
Sensitivity to 1% change in risk-free interest rate. Calls have positive Rho; puts negative. Least impactful for short-dated options.
Call: positive · Put: negative
⚖
Put-Call Parity
Mathematical identity: C − P = S·e−qT − K·e−rT. Ensures no arbitrage. If violated in real markets, traders immediately exploit it.
Must hold in efficient markets
